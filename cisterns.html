Cisterns

A cistern is a waterproof receptacle for holding liquids, usually water. Cisterns are often used to collect and store rainwater for irrigation, toilet flushing, and other non-potable purposes. They are typically designed to capture and store rainwater from rooftops, pavements, and other impervious surfaces, and can be made of a variety of materials, including concrete, steel, and plastic. Cisterns can be used in a variety of settings, including residential, commercial, and industrial areas, and can be an effective way to conserve water and reduce stormwater runoff.

Types of Cisterns

1. Underground Cisterns:
These are buried underground and are often used for storing large quantities of water.
2. Above-Ground Cisterns:
These are located above ground and are often used for smaller-scale water storage.
3. Rainwater Harvesting Cisterns:
These are specifically designed to collect and store rainwater for later use.
4. Concrete Cisterns:
These are made of concrete and are often used for large-scale water storage.

Benefits of Cisterns

1. Water Conservation:
Cisterns help to conserve water by collecting and storing rainwater for later use.
2. Reduced Stormwater Runoff:
Cisterns can help to reduce stormwater runoff by capturing and storing rainwater.
3. Low Maintenance:
Cisterns are relatively low maintenance and can be easily cleaned and inspected.
4. Cost-Effective:
Cisterns can be a cost-effective way to collect and store water, especially for irrigation and toilet flushing.
